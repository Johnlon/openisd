# OpenISD

### *Speaker design belongs to everyone who builds.*

---

For half a century, the math that turns a driver and a box into music has been
real, settled, and public. Thiele and Small handed it to the world in the 1970s.
And yet today, if you want to design an enclosure, your choices are a Windows
binary abandoned in 2016, a sprawling tool that won't run on your laptop, a
DOS-era interface, or a web calculator that quietly gives you the wrong answer
at the frequencies that matter.

The knowledge is open. The tools are not.

**OpenISD exists to close that gap.** A modern, fast, trustworthy loudspeaker
simulator that runs anywhere there's a browser — phone, laptop, workshop tablet —
with no install, no licence key, no dead end. Open source. Built in the open.
Owned by the people who use it.

---

## What we believe

**1. Trust is the whole game.**
A simulator that's pretty but wrong is worse than useless — it costs people real
money in drivers and timber. Every model in OpenISD is validated against the
closed-form physics and proves it to you on load. We show our work. If the
numbers are wrong, the test fails, in public, and we fix it. No black boxes.

**2. Friction is the enemy.**
The best tool is the one you can open right now. One file, one URL, zero setup.
If a fourteen-year-old with a curiosity and a hand-me-down laptop can't run it in
five seconds, we've failed. Power should not require a 200 MB download and a
Windows VM.

**3. Driver data should be a commons, not a moat.**
The reason people stayed chained to dead software was never the software — it was
the driver library. So we're building that library *together*, in the open,
version-controlled, correctable by anyone. Every spec sheet you import is a gift
to the next builder. No one should re-key the same Thiele-Small parameters a
thousand times in a thousand private files.

**4. A design is meant to be shared.**
Your enclosure should be a link you can paste into a forum, not a proprietary file
nobody else can open. Knowledge compounds when it's passed around. OpenISD is
built so that sharing a design — and improving someone else's — is the natural
thing to do.

**5. The community owns the roadmap.**
This is not a product with users. It's a workshop with members. MIT-licensed, so
it can never be taken away, closed up, or left to rot behind a login. If we
disappear, you fork it and carry on. That's the point.

---

## What this is not

Not a startup. Not a freemium funnel. Not a walled garden with a "Pro" tier
dangling the features you actually need. Not another tool that dies when one
maintainer loses interest.

It's the calculator the speaker-building world should have had a decade ago, and
it belongs to all of us.

---

## Join us

You don't need to be an acoustician. You need to care that this exists.

- **Builders** — use it, break it, tell us where the numbers feel wrong.
- **Hoarders of spec sheets** — bring your driver files. Seed the commons.
- **Coders** — it's plain HTML and JavaScript, no build step. A box type or a
  filter is a weekend and a pull request.
- **Teachers, writers, evangelists** — help people find it.

The physics is free. The tools should be too.

**Pull up a bench. Let's build the thing we all needed.**
